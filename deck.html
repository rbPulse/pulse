02 · The Problem

The category has access.
It doesn't have experience.

Today, anyone pursuing peptides has to become their own pharmacist, nurse, and clinician. That's not a wellness product — that's a chemistry project.
01
Reconstitution
Lyophilized powder + bacteriostatic water + manual math. One mistake ruins the vial.
02
Manual injection
Multi-vials, multi-syringes, daily self-injection with no guardrails. Adherence collapses within weeks.
03
No oversight
Gray-market suppliers, no prescription, no clinician, no titration, no safety net.
04
Trust gap
Compound quality varies wildly. Consumers can't distinguish pharmaceutical-grade from research chemicals.

08 · Business Model

Subscription by design.

Peptides are a protocol, not a purchase. Members pay a monthly membership that covers their protocol, clinician care, and delivery — with an ongoing relationship that deepens over time.
Revenue
Monthly membership
Protocol fee + clinician care + hardware + ongoing support, bundled into a single recurring charge.
Unit Economics
Premium, sticky, high LTV
Protocols run 8–24 weeks minimum. Cross-sell across categories (recovery, sleep, shred, growth). Multi-protocol members drive LTV expansion.
Moat
Hardware + brand
Once a member owns the pen and trusts the protocol, switching cost is high. The brand becomes the trust layer in a category with none.
Expansion
Stacks & seasonality
Members cycle through protocols by goal and season. The platform enables repeat purchases and category expansion without friction.
