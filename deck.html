02 · Problem

The category is broken
at the product layer.

Every incumbent — from gray-market suppliers to DTC telehealth — has failed to solve the core friction: peptides demand a level of clinical sophistication consumers don't have, and no one has built product to close the gap.
FRICTION 01
Compounding & reconstitution
Lyophilized peptides require bacteriostatic water, precise math, and sterile handling. Most users fail the first step.
FRICTION 02
Adherence collapse
Injection friction and variable dose experience drive drop-off. Average user abandons protocol within 30 days.
FRICTION 03
Absent clinical oversight
Gray-market access means no prescription, no titration, no monitoring, and no safety escalation path.
FRICTION 04
No quality standard
Consumers cannot distinguish compounded pharmaceutical-grade from research-chemical resellers. Trust is the bottleneck.
03 · Insight

The market is confusing
access with experience.

Every competitor has focused on solving access — cheaper compounds, faster shipping, more SKUs. But in a category defined by compounded, daily-use injectables, experience is the moat. Pulse is built around that insight.
What others built
A supply chain.
Gray-market resellers, DTC telehealth, compounding pharmacies — all compete on access and price. The product experience is unchanged from research-chemical days.
What Pulse built
A category.
Proprietary hardware, clinical oversight, branded member experience. We own the product layer the entire category is missing — the defensible layer.
04 · Product

Vertically integrated
by design.

Four layers. One platform. Every layer is proprietary or exclusive — creating compounding advantage as each piece scales.
HARDWARE
Pre-filled pods + reusable auto-injector pen
Exclusive partnership with medical device manufacturer. Eliminates reconstitution, measurement, and dosing errors. Enables insulin-pen simplicity in a category that has never had it.
CLINICAL
MSO / PC structure with proprietary protocols
Physician-owned professional corporation handles clinical decisions. Pulse MSO owns technology, brand, and operations. Regulatory compliance used by category leaders (Ro, Hims, Hone).
PHARMACY
503A compounding pharmacy partnership
Pharmaceutical-grade, prescription-only, third-party lab verified. Direct-to-member shipping. Quality standard absent across the rest of the market.
PLATFORM
Member + clinician portals
Proprietary software stack: intake evaluation, adherence tracking, live messaging, scheduled check-ins, protocol titration. The relationship layer competitors don't own.

07 · Business Model

High-margin subscription.
Multi-protocol expansion.

Peptides are protocols, not purchases. Members pay a recurring membership that bundles pharmacy, clinician, hardware, and software — with natural expansion into adjacent protocols.
REVENUE
Recurring membership
Monthly subscription covers protocol, clinician care, and ongoing support. Targeting premium ARPU consistent with Hims, Ro, and Hone pricing.
UNIT ECONOMICS
Premium LTV, low churn
Protocols run 8–24 weeks minimum. Hardware lock-in reduces switching. Clinical relationship creates stickiness incumbent DTC brands don't have.
EXPANSION
Multi-protocol cross-sell
Recovery, sleep, growth, shred, metabolic — members cycle through adjacent protocols seasonally, compounding LTV without increasing CAC.
MARGIN
Software-like economics
Compounded peptides carry premium margins vs. branded pharma. Clinical network is variable, not fixed. Gross margin profile trends toward SaaS as scale increases.
